Camping

I found my love of camping through my Father. Each year he takes my mom, siblings, and I up to the Deadwood Reservoir in the Sawtooth Mountains. We always head up early June, right when the snow melts. Since we go up so early, we are usually the only people up there and it is secluded and quiet - just how we like it! Each day is filled with fishing the streams and reservoir for trout. In the evenings, we make our traditional Bananna Boats! It is not very well know, but our family loves them! I will include the recipe and instructions below.

Bananna Boat Recipe

  1. First, gather your ingredients
    • Banannas
    • Small marsh-mallows
    • Chocolate Chips
    • Red Hots
    • Any other toppins desired
    • Aluminum Foil

  2. Secondly, take the bannana - don't peel it - and make a deep slip through the top to the bottom of the bannana. Sort of like a taco.
    • Warning: don't cut all the way through or else while cooking it will spill out

  3. Next, pull the bannana apart and stuff if with the desired fillings. I prefer chocolate, marshmellows, and redhots
    • Other Possible fillings include:
      • Crushed Grahm Crackers
      • Cookie Pieces
      • Peanut Butter
      • Nutella

  4. After that, wrap the stuffed bananna tightly in aluminum foil. Put it onto the coals of the fire and let it sit for 5-10 minutes

  5. Lastly, carefully remove from fire and enjoy!
